**"Natural" doesn't always mean "neutral."** If you are managing high blood pressure (hypertension), some herbs are not just unhelpfulâ€”they are actively dangerous. They can counteract your medication, retain fluid, or directly constrict your blood vessels. Knowing which ones to avoid is just as important as knowing which ones to take.

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**Licorice Root** (*Glycyrrhiza glabra*) is the biggest offender (Grade A Evidence). It mimics a hormone that makes your body hold onto salt and water, driving blood pressure up fast. **Ephedra** (Ma Huang) and **Yohimbe** are powerful stimulants that can cause a "hypertensive crisis" and should be strictly avoided.

> **The Golden Rule:** If it gives you "energy" or "jitters," check your blood pressure. Stimulants are generally risky for hypertension until proven safe.

### 1. Licorice Root: The Sodium Trap ðŸ¬
*(Glycyrrhiza glabra)* | **Evidence Level: A**

*   **The Danger:** Licorice contains *glycyrrhizin*, which blocks the enzyme that breaks down cortisol. This causes your kidneys to hoard sodium and dump potassium. The result? Fluid retention and a sharp rise in blood pressure.
*   **The Trap:** It's hidden everywhereâ€”in cough teas, digestive blends, and even candy.
*   **The Fix:** Look for **DGL (Deglycyrrhizinated Licorice)**. It has the soothing benefits for your stomach *without* the blood pressure spike.

### 2. Ephedra & Yohimbe: The Adrenaline Spikes ðŸš€
*(Ephedra sinica / Pausinystalia yohimbe)* | **Evidence Level: A**

*   **The Danger:** These are "sympathomimetics"â€”they mimic adrenaline. They clamp down on your blood vessels (vasoconstriction) and race your heart.
*   **The Risk:** Stroke, heart attack, and hypertensive crisis. Ephedra is banned in many places for this exact reason.
*   **Where They Hide:** Weight loss supplements, "energy" pills, and libido boosters.

### 3. Panax Ginseng: The Wild Card ðŸ‰
*(Panax ginseng)* | **Evidence Level: B**

*   **The Nuance:** While some studies show it helps, others show it raises blood pressure (the "Ginseng Abuse Syndrome"). It can be too stimulating for some.
*   **Verdict:** Use with caution. Monitor your BP daily if you start it. **American Ginseng** (*Panax quinquefolius*) is generally cooler and safer.

## âš ï¸ Safety & "Need to Know"

*   **Medication Interaction:** Licorice can make diuretic medications (water pills) dangerous by depleting potassium too low (hypokalemia).
*   **Candy Counts:** Real black licorice candy contains enough glycyrrhizin to send you to the ER if you eat it daily. Check the label for "anise flavor" (safe) vs. "licorice extract" (unsafe).
*   **Salt Sensitivity:** If you are salt-sensitive, you are likely *more* sensitive to Licorice.
