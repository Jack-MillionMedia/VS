# Q25: Which Herbs Can Worsen High Blood Pressure Instead of Improving It?

**Quick Answer:** Licorice root (*Glycyrrhiza glabra*) is the most well-documented herb that can worsen high blood pressure (Grade A evidence - causes significant BP elevation). Stimulant herbs like ephedra (banned but still found in some products) and high-dose caffeine can also increase blood pressure. The key is avoiding these herbs if you have hypertension and being aware of hidden sources (licorice in teas, candies, supplements).

---

## Detailed Evidence-Based Answer

### Understanding Blood Pressure Effects

**Herbs that INCREASE blood pressure:**
- Stimulants (increase heart rate and BP)
- Licorice (causes sodium retention)
- Some adaptogens (if too stimulating)

**Herbs that DECREASE blood pressure:**
- Hawthorn, hibiscus, garlic, olive leaf (covered in Q9)

**Critical:** Some herbs can worsen hypertension, making it important to know which to avoid.

## Herbs That WORSEN High Blood Pressure

### 1. Licorice Root (*Glycyrrhiza glabra*) - Grade A Evidence

**Risk:** üî¥ **HIGH - SIGNIFICANT BP ELEVATION**

**Evidence Summary:**
- **Blood Pressure:** Grade A evidence - Multiple studies show significant BP elevation
- **Mechanism:** Well-established - causes sodium retention and potassium loss
- **Hypertension:** Can cause or worsen hypertension
- **Dose-Dependent:** Higher doses = greater risk

**How It Works:**
- Glycyrrhizin ‚Üí inhibits 11-beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase
- Increases cortisol activity (mineralocorticoid effects)
- Causes sodium retention
- Causes potassium loss (hypokalemia)
- Increases blood volume ‚Üí increases BP

**Dosing That Causes Problems:**
- **As little as 50-100mg glycyrrhizin daily** can cause BP elevation
- **Chronic use** (weeks to months) increases risk
- **Individual variation** - Some people more sensitive

**CRITICAL SAFETY:**
- üî¥ **AVOID if you have hypertension** - Can significantly worsen BP
- üî¥ **AVOID long-term use** - Even in normotensive individuals
- üî¥ **Monitor BP** if using licorice-containing products
- ‚úÖ **DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice)** - Safe alternative (glycyrrhizin removed)

**Timeline:** BP elevation can occur within days to weeks of regular use

**Individual Variation:** Some people more sensitive than others.

### 2. Ephedra (*Ephedra sinica*) - BANNED but Still Found

**Risk:** üî¥ **HIGH - SIGNIFICANT BP ELEVATION**

**Evidence Summary:**
- **Blood Pressure:** Grade A evidence - Significant BP elevation
- **Cardiovascular Risk:** Multiple case reports of serious cardiovascular events
- **Banned:** FDA banned ephedra-containing supplements (2004)
- **Still Found:** May still be found in some products (especially imported)

**How It Works:**
- Stimulant (ephedrine alkaloids)
- Increases heart rate
- Increases blood pressure
- Vasoconstriction
- Significant cardiovascular risk

**CRITICAL SAFETY:**
- üî¥ **BANNED in US** - FDA banned ephedra-containing supplements
- üî¥ **ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION** - Especially with hypertension
- üî¥ **Life-threatening** - Can cause heart attack, stroke

**Timeline:** Immediate BP elevation

### 3. High-Dose Caffeine (Coffee, Tea, Supplements)

**Risk:** üü° **MODERATE - TEMPORARY BP ELEVATION**

**Evidence Summary:**
- **Blood Pressure:** Grade B evidence - Temporary BP elevation (5-10 mmHg)
- **Acute Effects:** Increases BP for 1-3 hours after consumption
- **Chronic Use:** Tolerance develops, but acute effects remain
- **Individual Variation:** Some people more sensitive

**How It Works:**
- Stimulant (increases heart rate)
- Vasoconstriction
- Increases BP temporarily
- Effects wear off within hours

**Dosing That Causes Problems:**
- **High doses:** >200-300mg caffeine (2-3 cups coffee)
- **Acute effects:** Even regular coffee drinkers experience temporary BP elevation
- **Individual sensitivity:** Some people more affected

**CRITICAL SAFETY:**
- üü° **Temporary elevation** - Usually resolves within hours
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Monitor if hypertensive** - May need to limit caffeine
- ‚úÖ **Moderate amounts usually OK** - 1-2 cups coffee typically fine
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Energy drinks** - Often contain high caffeine + other stimulants

**Timeline:** BP elevation within 30-60 minutes; resolves within 1-3 hours

## Hidden Sources of Problem Herbs

### Licorice Found In:
- **Herbal teas** - Many blends contain licorice
- **Candies** - Black licorice candy
- **Supplements** - Some digestive, respiratory, or throat products
- **Traditional formulas** - Chinese medicine formulas often contain licorice
- **Read labels** - Check for "Glycyrrhiza glabra" or "licorice root"

**Safe Alternative:** DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice) - Glycyrrhizin removed, safe for BP

---

### Ephedra Found In:
- **Imported products** - May still be found in some imported supplements
- **"Natural" stimulants** - Some weight loss or energy products
- **Check labels** - Look for "ephedra," "ma huang," "ephedrine"

**CRITICAL:** Ephedra is banned - avoid any product containing it

## Clinical Pearls

1. **Licorice is High-Risk:** Can significantly worsen hypertension - avoid
2. **DGL is Safe:** Deglycyrrhizinated licorice doesn't affect BP
3. **Check Labels:** Licorice hidden in many products
4. **Ephedra is Banned:** Still found in some products - avoid completely
5. **Caffeine Temporary:** Usually OK in moderation, but monitor
6. **Individual Variation:** Some people more sensitive to stimulants
7. **Monitor BP:** If using any stimulant herbs, monitor BP regularly

## When to See a Professional

üö® **Seek medical help if:**

**Always monitor BP when using herbs, especially if you have hypertension.**
