**"I want to fight the cancer with everything I've got."** It's a natural instinct. But chemotherapy is a highly calibrated chemical weapon. Some herbs act as "human shields" for the cancer cells, protecting them from the treatment, while others flush the drugs out of your system too fast. During active treatment, **less is often more.**

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**The Golden Rule: Ask your Oncologist.**
1.  **ABSOLUTE NO:** **St. John's Wort.** It induces liver enzymes (CYP3A4) that metabolize chemotherapy drugs. It literally "eats" the chemo, leading to treatment failure.
2.  **PROBABLY NO:** **High-Dose Antioxidants** (Vitamin C IVs, Green Tea Extract). Chemo often works by creating "oxidative stress" to kill cancer cells. Antioxidants might neutralize this effect, protecting the tumor.
3.  **YES (With Approval):** **Ginger.** It is the gold standard for chemo-induced nausea and does *not* interfere with drug metabolism.

> **The Reality Check:** You cannot "boost" your immune system *during* chemo in the way you think. Chemo is designed to suppress fast-growing cells (including immune cells). Fighting against the chemo with immune stimulants (like Echinacea) can be counterproductive.

*(Vitamins C, E, A, Green Tea)* | **Evidence Level: B (Controversial)**

*   **Verdict:** **Eat food, skip the pills.** A blueberry is fine; a 1000mg Vitamin C supplement is not recommended during infusion days.

## âš ï¸ Safety & "Need to Know"

*   **Blood Clots:** Cancer increases the risk of blood clots. Herbs like **Ginkgo** and **Garlic** thin the blood. If your platelets are low from chemo, these herbs can cause dangerous bleeding.
*   **Hormone Sensitive Cancers:** If you have Estrogen-Positive (ER+) breast cancer, avoid "phytoestrogens" like **Red Clover** or **Dong Quai** unless cleared by your oncologist.
