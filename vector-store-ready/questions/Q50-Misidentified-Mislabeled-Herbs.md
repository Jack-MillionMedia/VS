# Q50: Which Herbs are Most Frequently Misidentified or Mislabeled by Online Sellers?

**Quick Answer:** Several herbs are **frequently misidentified or mislabeled** by online sellers, posing serious safety risks. **Ginkgo biloba** is often adulterated with other plants or contains incorrect plant parts (Grade B evidence). **St. John's Wort** is frequently misidentified or adulterated with other Hypericum species (Grade B evidence). **Echinacea** is commonly mislabeled with different Echinacea species or other plants (Grade B evidence). **Ginseng** is frequently adulterated with cheaper substitutes or contains incorrect species (Grade B evidence). **Turmeric** is often adulterated with cheaper fillers or incorrect species (Grade B evidence). Always buy from **reputable suppliers** with third-party testing and authentication.

---

## Detailed Evidence-Based Answer

### Understanding Herb Misidentification and Mislabeling

**Misidentification** occurs when:
- **Wrong species** - Different plant sold as desired herb
- **Wrong plant part** - Root sold as leaf, or vice versa
- **Incorrect variety** - Different variety with different effects

**Mislabeling** occurs when:
- **Adulteration** - Cheaper substances added (fillers, other plants)
- **Substitution** - Cheaper herb substituted for expensive one
- **Contamination** - Pesticides, heavy metals, other contaminants

**Risks:**
- **Safety concerns** - Wrong herb may be toxic or interact with medications
- **Ineffectiveness** - Wrong herb won't provide desired effects
- **Wasted money** - Paying for something you're not getting
- **Health complications** - Adulterants may cause harm

**Prevalence:** Studies show **30-50% of herbal products** may be mislabeled or adulterated, especially from online sellers without quality control.

## How to Avoid Misidentified/Mislabeled Herbs

### 1. Buy from Reputable Suppliers
- **Established brands** with quality control
- **Third-party testing** (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab)
- **Transparent sourcing** - Know where herbs come from
- **Avoid random online sellers** - Especially Amazon third-party sellers

### 2. Look for Quality Indicators
- **Standardized extracts** - Specific active compound percentages
- **Third-party verification** - USP, NSF, ConsumerLab seals
- **COA (Certificate of Analysis)** - Available upon request
- **GMP certification** - Good Manufacturing Practices

### 3. Red Flags to Avoid
- üî¥ **Unusually low prices** - Too good to be true usually is
- üî¥ **No third-party testing** - No verification of quality
- üî¥ **Vague labeling** - "Proprietary blend" without specifics
- üî¥ **No company information** - Can't verify manufacturer
- üî¥ **Amazon third-party sellers** - High risk of counterfeits

### 4. Trusted Brands (Examples)

- **NOW Foods** - Third-party tested, GMP certified
