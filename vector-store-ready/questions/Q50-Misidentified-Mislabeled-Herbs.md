**You ordered Ginseng, but what's in the bottle?** DNA testing of herbal products has revealed a shocking truth: nearly 30-50% of herbal products sold online are mislabeled, adulterated, or contain entirely different plants than what is listed on the label. Some herbs are "faked" more than others.

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**Buyer Beware of the "Big 5."**
1.  **Ginkgo Biloba:** The #1 most adulterated herb. Often just cheap fillers spiked with rutin (a chemical) to trick lab tests.
2.  **St. John's Wort:** Frequently substituted with other *Hypericum* species that have zero antidepressant effect.
3.  **Ginseng:** Expensive Asian Ginseng (*Panax ginseng*) is often swapped for cheaper American Ginseng or just caffeine powder.
4.  **Echinacea:** The root is the medicine, but cheap brands use the leaves (which do nothing).
5.  **Black Cohosh:** Often swapped for Chinese Actaea, which can be liver-toxic.

> **The Golden Rule:** **If it's cheap, it's fake.** Real Ginseng takes 6 years to grow. Real Saffron costs more than gold. If you find a "bargain" on these, you are buying sawdust.
