**"It's just tea, how can it hurt?"** If you suffer from seasonal hay fever (Ragweed allergy), your immune system is on high alert for specific protein structures. Unfortunately, some popular medicinal herbs are botanical cousins to Ragweed and share those exact same proteins. Drinking them can trick your body into thinking you just swallowed a bouquet of pollen.

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**Beware the Aster Family.**
The *Asteraceae* (Daisy) family is the biggest culprit. If you are allergic to **Ragweed**, **Chrysanthemums**, or **Marigolds**, you have a higher risk of reacting to:
1.  **Chamomile** (The most common trigger)
2.  **Echinacea**
3.  **Dandelion**
4.  **Feverfew**
5.  **Milk Thistle**

> **The Reality Check:** **It's Rare but Real.** Most people with ragweed allergies can drink Chamomile tea just fine. But for the sensitive 1-5%, it can cause hives, throat swelling, or even anaphylaxis. Proceed with caution.
