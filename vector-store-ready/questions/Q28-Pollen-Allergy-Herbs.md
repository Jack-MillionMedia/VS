# Q28: Are there Herbs that Can Cause Allergic Reactions Similar to Pollen Allergies?

**Quick Answer:** Yes, herbs in the Asteraceae (Compositae) family can cause allergic reactions in people allergic to ragweed and related plants due to cross-reactivity. Chamomile (*Matricaria chamomilla*) and echinacea (*Echinacea spp.*) are the most common culprits, though reactions are rare (<1-5% of people). Other Asteraceae herbs (dandelion, calendula, yarrow) may also cause reactions. The key is knowing your allergies and testing herbs cautiously if you have ragweed/pollen allergies.

## Testing for Allergies

### Safe Testing Protocol:

**Step 1: Skin Test**
- Apply small amount of herb (tea or diluted tincture) to inner forearm
- Wait 24 hours
- Watch for redness, itching, swelling

**Step 2: Small Internal Test**
- If skin test OK, try very small amount internally
- Start with 1/4 normal dose
- Wait 24-48 hours
- Watch for any reactions

**Step 3: Gradual Increase**
- If no reaction, gradually increase to normal dose
- Monitor for any symptoms

**CRITICAL:** If any reaction occurs, discontinue immediately.

## Recognizing Allergic Reactions

### Mild Reactions:
- Skin rash (especially around mouth/face)
- Itching
- Mild swelling
- Hives

### Moderate Reactions:
- More widespread rash
- Significant swelling
- Difficulty swallowing
- Nausea

### Severe Reactions (Anaphylaxis - Medical Emergency):
- Difficulty breathing
- Swelling of throat/face
- Rapid heart rate
- Dizziness/fainting
- **Call 911 immediately**
