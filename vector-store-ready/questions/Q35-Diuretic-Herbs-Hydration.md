**"I just want to lose water weight."** Be careful. Diuretics force your kidneys to work overtime. While some herbs are gentle and nourishing, others work by irritating the delicate kidney tissue to provoke a reaction. Using the wrong one long-term can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances.

**Dandelion Leaf** (*Taraxacum officinale*) is the safest and smartest choice. Unlike pharmaceutical diuretics (like Lasix) that strip your body of potassium, Dandelion is naturally high in potassium, replacing exactly what you lose (Grade B Evidence). **Juniper Berry** and **Uva Ursi** are strong diuretics but should only be used for acute infections (like UTIs), not for daily water retention, as they can stress the kidneys.

> **The Golden Rule:** **Leaf for Kidneys, Root for Liver.** Dandelion *Leaf* is the diuretic. Dandelion *Root* is for liver detox. If you want to pee, pick the leaf.
