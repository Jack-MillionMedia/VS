**Taking the right herb at the wrong time is like drinking espresso before a nap.** You wouldn't chug a Red Bull at 10 PM, yet many people unknowingly take stimulating herbs right before bed. The result? A "tired but wired" brain that refuses to shut down.

**Yes!** Energizing adaptogens like **Rhodiola** and **Ginseng** mimic the body's "wake up" signals and should be taken **before 2 PM**. Caffeine-containing herbs (Green Tea, Guarana) have a 5-hour half-life and should be avoided **after 2 PM**.

> **The Golden Rule:** Energize in the AM. Relax in the PM.

### 1. The "False Friends" (Healthy but Stimulating)
*   **Rhodiola Rosea:** It boosts dopamine and norepinephrine. Great for a morning meeting, terrible for a bedtime story.
*   **Panax Ginseng:** Known for increasing alertness and physical energy. Taking this at night is asking for a restless sleep.
*   **B-Complex Vitamins:** Not an herb, but often found in herbal blends. They fuel cellular energy production and can be surprisingly stimulating.

### 2. The "Hidden Caffeine"
*   **Green Tea / Matcha:** Contains L-Theanine (calming) but also Caffeine (stimulating). The caffeine usually wins in the battle for sleep.
*   **Chocolate / Cocoa:** Yes, your "calming" hot cocoa has theobromine and caffeine, which are stimulants!
