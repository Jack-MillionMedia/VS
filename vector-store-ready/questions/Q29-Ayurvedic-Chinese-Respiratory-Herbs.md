**Same lungs, different languages.** Both Ayurveda (India) and Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) have treated respiratory illness for 5,000 years, but their strategies are distinct. Understanding the difference helps you pick the right tool for *your* body type.

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**Ayurveda = The Fire Starter.** It views congestion as cold, heavy, and damp ("Kapha"). The cure is to **Heat It Up**. It uses pungent, spicy herbs like **Ginger**, **Black Pepper**, and **Tulsi** to melt the mucus and reignite digestion ("Agni").

**TCM = The Shield Builder.** It views respiratory weakness as a deficiency of "Lung Qi" (energy) or an invasion of "Wind-Cold." The cure is to **Strengthen the Wall**. It uses tonics like **Astragalus** and **Cordyceps** to build immunity and **Licorice** to harmonize the lungs.

> **The Golden Rule:** **Don't Tonic a Cold.** In TCM, you never take immune builders (Astragalus) *during* a fever. It's like locking the burglar inside the house. Use Ayurveda's spicy approach to "sweat it out" first, then use TCM to rebuild.
