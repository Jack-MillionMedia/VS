**Not all coughs are created equal.** A dry, hacking cough needs soothing. A wet, heavy cough needs clearing. Using the wrong herb can actually make things worse (like suppressing a cough that *needs* to get mucus out). The key is to match the plant to the problem.

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**Thyme** (*Thymus vulgaris*) is the champion for **Wet/Productive Coughs**. It acts as an expectorant (loosens mucus) and an antispasmodic (stops the spasm), making it perfect for bronchitis (Grade B Evidence). **Mullein** (*Verbascum thapsus*) is the go-to for **Dry/Irritated Coughs**. It coats the throat and calms the urge to hack. **Elderberry** is your first line of defense against the viral infection itself.

> **The Golden Rule:** **Steam is Supreme.** For immediate congestion relief, nothing beats a steam inhalation with **Eucalyptus** or **Thyme**. It delivers the medicine directly to your lungs, bypassing digestion entirely.
