**Why would a cough syrup herb also work for a UTI?** It seems counterintuitive, but in herbal medicine, the lungs and the kidneys are "sister organs." Both are elimination pathways, both are lined with mucous membranes, and both are susceptible to bacterial infections. Certain plants have mastered the art of healing both at once.

## âš¡ The Quick Answer
**Volatile Oils are the Key.**
Herbs like **Thyme**, **Goldenrod**, and **Eucalyptus** are rich in essential oils. When you swallow them (as tea or tincture), these oils enter your bloodstream and are specifically eliminated through two exits: your breath (lungs) and your urine (kidneys). As they exit, they disinfect the tissue on contact.
*   **Thyme:** Clears bronchitis *and* disinfects the bladder.
*   **Goldenrod:** Dries up sinus allergies *and* flushes out kidney stones.

> **The Golden Rule:** **"If you can smell it in your pee, it's working."** Asparagus isn't the only one. When you take a strong dose of Thyme or Juniper, you might smell it in your urine. That means the antimicrobial oils have successfully reached your bladder.
