**A weak stream doesn't always mean you need a diuretic.** In fact, if the problem is an enlarged prostate (BPH) or inflammation, taking a strong diuretic (like caffeine or Juniper) can make things *worse* by irritating the bladder. The goal is to open the channel, not just increase the water pressure.

**Yes.** You want "Aquaretics" or "Anti-inflammatories," not strong diuretics.
1.  **Corn Silk** (*Zea mays*) is a gentle aquaretic. It increases flow slightly but mostly *soothes* the urinary tract, allowing muscles to relax and flow to happen naturally (Grade B Evidence).
2.  **Saw Palmetto** (*Serenoa repens*) works mechanically. It shrinks the swollen prostate tissue that is pinching the urethra, restoring flow without affecting your hydration levels at all (Grade A Evidence).

> **The Golden Rule:** **Hydrate to Flow.** It sounds contradictory, but drinking *less* water makes urine concentrated and irritating, which causes spasms and *worse* flow. Drink normal amounts of water and use herbs to relax the exit.
