# Q44: How do I Differentiate Between Authentic and Adulterated Herbal Supplements?

**Quick Answer:** Differentiating authentic from adulterated herbal supplements requires multiple strategies: buy from reputable companies with third-party testing (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab certifications), check for standardization and proper labeling, avoid suspiciously cheap products, research the company's quality control practices, and be aware of common adulteration risks (turmeric with lead chromate, ginkgo with other species, St. John's Wort with other plants). The key is buying from trusted sources with verified quality control - price is often an indicator of quality.

---

## Detailed Evidence-Based Answer

### Understanding Adulteration Risks

**Types of Adulteration:**
- **Species substitution** - Wrong plant species
- **Dilution** - Product mixed with fillers
- **Contamination** - Heavy metals, pesticides, microbes
- **False labeling** - Claims don't match contents
- **Additives** - Undisclosed ingredients

**Why It Matters:**
- **Safety** - Adulterated products can be dangerous
- **Efficacy** - Adulterated products may not work
- **Waste of money** - Paying for inferior products

Different herbs have different adulteration risks.
