# Pathophysiology Reference: Respiratory & Immune System Conditions

**Context:** This monograph provides an advanced pathophysiological analysis of respiratory and autoimmune disorders. It focuses on the molecular mechanisms of hypersensitivity, chronic inflammation, and immune tolerance breakdown.

---

## Asthma

### Etiology & Risk Factors
Chronic inflammatory disorder of the airways characterized by reversible obstruction and bronchial hyperresponsiveness.
*   **Phenotypes:**
    *   **Th2-High (Allergic):** Early onset, atopic, eosinophilic.
    *   **Th2-Low (Non-Allergic):** Late onset, obese, neutrophilic.
*   **Triggers:** Allergens (dust mites, pollen), viral infections (Rhinovirus), exercise, aspirin (AERD).

### Molecular & Cellular Pathogenesis
**Type I Hypersensitivity (IgE-Mediated):**
1.  **Sensitization:** APCs present allergen to Naive T-cells $\rightarrow$ differentiation into **Th2 cells** (driven by IL-4).
2.  **IgE Production:** Th2 cells secrete **IL-4** and **IL-13** $\rightarrow$ B-cell class switching to IgE.
3.  **Mast Cell Priming:** IgE binds to Fc$\epsilon$RI receptors on mast cells.
4.  **Early Phase Reaction (Minutes):** Allergen cross-links IgE $\rightarrow$ degranulation $\rightarrow$ release of **Histamine** (vasodilation, edema), **Leukotrienes** (LTC4/D4/E4 - potent bronchoconstrictors), and **Prostaglandins**.
5.  **Late Phase Reaction (Hours):** Th2 cells secrete **IL-5** $\rightarrow$ recruitment of **Eosinophils** $\rightarrow$ release of Major Basic Protein (MBP) $\rightarrow$ epithelial damage and airway remodeling.

### Systemic Pathophysiology
*   **Airway Remodeling:** Subepithelial fibrosis, smooth muscle hypertrophy, goblet cell hyperplasia (mucus hypersecretion).
*   **Airflow Obstruction:** Bronchospasm + Mucosal Edema + Mucus Plugs.
*   **Gas Exchange:** V/Q mismatch (shunt) $\rightarrow$ Hypoxemia. Hyperventilation initially causes respiratory alkalosis; fatigue leads to respiratory acidosis (ominous sign).

### Pathophysiological Cascade
**[Flow: Airway Obstruction & Hypoxemia]**
1.  **Initial Insult:** **Bronchospasm** (smooth muscle contraction) + **Mucosal Edema**.
2.  **Compensatory Response:** **Hyperventilation**. Respiratory drive increases to blow off CO2.
3.  **Maladaptive Consequence:**
    *   **Air Trapping:** Air enters during inspiration (airways dilate) but is trapped during expiration (airways collapse/narrow).
    *   **Dynamic Hyperinflation:** Lungs overfill $\rightarrow$ diaphragm flattens $\rightarrow$ mechanical disadvantage.
4.  **Symptom Mechanism:**
    *   **Wheezing:** Turbulent airflow through narrowed bronchi (Bernoulli's principle).
    *   **Pulsus Paradoxus:** Hyperinflated lungs compress the heart during inspiration $\rightarrow$ reduced LV filling $\rightarrow$ drop in stroke volume/BP.

### Clinical Correlations
*   **Triad:** Dyspnea, Wheezing, Cough (worse at night).
*   **Pulsus Paradoxus:** >10 mmHg drop in SBP during inspiration (severe air trapping).
*   **Samter's Triad:** Asthma + Nasal Polyps + Aspirin Sensitivity.

### Diagnostic Biomarkers
*   **Spirometry:** FEV1/FVC < 0.70, reversible with bronchodilator (>12% increase).
*   **FeNO:** Fractional exhaled Nitric Oxide (marker of eosinophilic inflammation).
*   **Sputum Eosinophils:** >2-3%.

## Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

### Etiology
Persistent respiratory symptoms and airflow limitation due to airway and/or alveolar abnormalities.
*   **Primary Cause:** Cigarette smoking (85-90%).
*   **Genetic:** $\alpha$-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (panacinar emphysema).

### Molecular & Cellular Pathogenesis
**Chronic Inflammation & Protease-Antiprotease Imbalance:**
1.  **Inflammatory Infiltrate:** Macrophages, Neutrophils, CD8+ T-cells (Tc1).
2.  **Oxidative Stress:** Cigarette smoke + ROS from neutrophils inactivates antiproteases (like $\alpha$-1 antitrypsin).
3.  **Tissue Destruction:** Neutrophil Elastase and MMPs (Matrix Metalloproteinases) degrade elastin and collagen.
    *   **Emphysema:** Destruction of alveolar walls $\rightarrow$ loss of elastic recoil $\rightarrow$ air trapping.
    *   **Chronic Bronchitis:** Mucous gland hypertrophy (Reid Index > 0.4) $\rightarrow$ mucus hypersecretion.

### Systemic Pathophysiology
*   **Air Trapping:** Hyperinflation $\rightarrow$ flattened diaphragm $\rightarrow$ mechanical disadvantage.
*   **Gas Exchange:**
    *   **Pink Puffer (Emphysema):** High V/Q (dead space), cachectic, normoxic until late.
    *   **Blue Bloater (Chronic Bronchitis):** Low V/Q (shunt), cyanotic, hypercapnic, cor pulmonale.
*   **Cor Pulmonale:** Chronic hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction $\rightarrow$ Pulmonary HTN $\rightarrow$ Right Heart Failure.

### Pathophysiological Cascade
**[Flow: Parenchymal Destruction & Gas Exchange Failure]**
1.  **Initial Insult:** **Elastin Destruction** (Emphysema) OR **Mucus Hypersecretion** (Bronchitis).
2.  **Compensatory Response:** **Pursed-Lip Breathing**. Creates positive back-pressure (auto-PEEP) to splint airways open during expiration.
3.  **Maladaptive Consequence:**
    *   **V/Q Mismatch:**
        *   Emphysema: Loss of surface area $\rightarrow$ diffusion limitation.
        *   Bronchitis: Shunt (perfusion without ventilation) $\rightarrow$ hypoxemia.
    *   **Hypoxic Vasoconstriction:** Low O2 in alveoli triggers pulmonary artery constriction $\rightarrow$ Pulmonary HTN.
4.  **Symptom Mechanism:**
    *   **Barrel Chest:** Chronic air trapping increases AP diameter.
    *   **Edema (Cor Pulmonale):** Right ventricle fails against high pulmonary pressure $\rightarrow$ systemic venous congestion.

### Clinical Correlations
*   **Barrel Chest:** Increased AP diameter.
*   **Pursed-Lip Breathing:** Increases auto-PEEP to prevent airway collapse.
*   **Digital Clubbing:** Sign of chronic hypoxemia (more common in cancer/fibrosis than pure COPD).

### Diagnostic Biomarkers
*   **Spirometry:** FEV1/FVC < 0.70 (post-bronchodilator), NOT fully reversible.
*   **ABG:** Chronic respiratory acidosis with metabolic compensation (high HCO3-).

## Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

### Etiology
Systemic autoimmune disease primarily targeting synovial joints.
*   **Genetics:** **HLA-DR4** (Shared Epitope).
*   **Triggers:** Smoking, Citrullination of proteins (PADI4 enzyme).

### Molecular & Cellular Pathogenesis
**Synovial Pannus Formation:**
1.  **Autoimmunity:** APCs present citrullinated peptides to CD4+ T-cells.
2.  **Autoantibodies:**
    *   **Rheumatoid Factor (RF):** IgM against Fc portion of IgG.
    *   **ACPA (Anti-CCP):** Antibodies against citrullinated proteins (highly specific).
3.  **Cytokine Storm:** Macrophages and Fibroblast-like Synoviocytes (FLS) secrete **TNF-$\alpha$**, **IL-1**, **IL-6**.
4.  **Joint Destruction:**
    *   **Pannus:** Invasive granulation tissue erodes cartilage and bone.
    *   **Osteoclasts:** Activated by RANKL (from T-cells/FLS) $\rightarrow$ bone erosion.

### Systemic Pathophysiology
*   **Joints:** Symmetrical polyarthritis (MCP, PIP, wrists). Morning stiffness > 1 hour.
*   **Extra-articular:**
    *   **Rheumatoid Nodules:** Fibrinoid necrosis surrounded by palisading histiocytes.
    *   **Vasculitis:** Inflammation of blood vessels.
    *   **Lung:** Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD), Caplan Syndrome (nodules + pneumoconiosis).

### Pathophysiological Cascade
**[Flow: Synovial Inflammation & Joint Destruction]**
1.  **Initial Insult:** **Autoimmune Attack** on synovium (Citrullinated proteins).
2.  **Compensatory Response:** **Pannus Formation**. Synovium proliferates (hyperplasia) to contain the inflammation.
3.  **Maladaptive Consequence:**
    *   **Cartilage Erosion:** Pannus releases MMPs that digest cartilage.
    *   **Bone Resorption:** RANKL expression activates osteoclasts.
4.  **Symptom Mechanism:**
    *   **Morning Stiffness:** Inflammatory edema accumulates in joint capsule overnight (gel phenomenon). Movement pumps fluid out via lymphatics.
    *   **Ulnar Deviation:** Tendon sheaths are damaged $\rightarrow$ extensor tendons slip off metacarpal heads.

### Clinical Correlations
*   **Deformities:** Ulnar deviation, Swan-neck deformity, Boutonniere deformity.
*   **Baker's Cyst:** Popliteal cyst rupture mimicking DVT.

### Diagnostic Biomarkers
*   **Serology:** **Anti-CCP** (High Specificity), RF (High Sensitivity), ANA.
*   **Inflammatory:** Elevated ESR, CRP.

## Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)

### Etiology
Chronic multisystem autoimmune disease with loss of tolerance to nuclear antigens.
*   **Demographics:** Female > Male (9:1), African American/Hispanic/Asian descent.
*   **Genetics:** HLA-DR2, HLA-DR3, Complement deficiencies (C1q, C2, C4).

### Molecular & Cellular Pathogenesis
**Immune Complex Deposition (Type III Hypersensitivity):**
1.  **Apoptosis Defect:** Impaired clearance of apoptotic cells releases nuclear antigens (DNA, histones).
2.  **Autoantibody Production:** B-cells produce ANAs (Anti-Nuclear Antibodies).
3.  **Immune Complexes:** Antigen-Antibody complexes form in circulation and deposit in tissues (kidneys, skin, joints).
4.  **Complement Activation:** Complexes activate classical complement pathway $\rightarrow$ inflammation (C3a, C5a) and tissue damage.

### Systemic Pathophysiology
*   **Kidney (Lupus Nephritis):** Glomerular deposition $\rightarrow$ nephrotic/nephritic syndrome. "Wire-loop" lesions.
*   **Skin:** Malar "Butterfly" rash (photosensitive), Discoid rash.
*   **Hematologic:** Cytopenias (Type II Hypersensitivity - direct antibody destruction).
*   **Cardiovascular:** Libman-Sacks Endocarditis (sterile vegetations on both sides of valve).

### Pathophysiological Cascade
**[Flow: Immune Complex Deposition & Tissue Injury]**
1.  **Initial Insult:** **Defective Apoptosis Clearance** $\rightarrow$ Nuclear antigens exposed.
2.  **Compensatory Response:** **Antibody Production**. B-cells produce anti-dsDNA to clear the "foreign" debris.
3.  **Maladaptive Consequence:** **Type III Hypersensitivity**. Antigen-Antibody complexes form in blood $\rightarrow$ deposit in basement membranes (kidney, skin).
4.  **Symptom Mechanism:**
    *   **Nephritis:** Complement activation (C5a) attracts neutrophils $\rightarrow$ glomerular inflammation $\rightarrow$ RBCs leak into urine (hematuria).
    *   **Malar Rash:** UV light induces apoptosis in keratinocytes $\rightarrow$ massive antigen release $\rightarrow$ local immune complex reaction.

### Clinical Correlations
*   **SOAP BRAIN MD:** **S**erositis, **O**ral ulcers, **A**rthritis, **P**hotosensitivity, **B**lood disorders, **R**enal, **A**NA, **I**mmunologic, **N**eurologic, **M**alar rash, **D**iscoid rash.

### Diagnostic Biomarkers
*   **Screening:** **ANA** (Sensitive but non-specific).
*   **Specific:** **Anti-dsDNA** (Specific, correlates with nephritis activity), **Anti-Smith** (Highly specific).
*   **Complement:** Low C3, C4 (consumed during active disease).

## Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

### Etiology
Autoimmune demyelinating disease of the CNS.
*   **Genetics:** HLA-DR2 (DRB1*1501).
*   **Environment:** Low Vitamin D, EBV infection, Smoking, Distance from equator.

### Molecular & Cellular Pathogenesis
**T-Cell Mediated Demyelination (Type IV Hypersensitivity):**
1.  **Peripheral Activation:** Autoreactive Th1 and Th17 cells activated against myelin antigens (MBP, MOG).
2.  **BBB Breakdown:** T-cells cross Blood-Brain Barrier (express VLA-4 integrin).
3.  **CNS Inflammation:** Reactivation by microglia. Cytokine release recruits macrophages.
4.  **Demyelination:** Macrophages strip myelin sheath $\rightarrow$ slowed conduction/conduction block.
5.  **Axonal Loss:** Permanent neurological deficit.

### Systemic Pathophysiology
*   **Lesions:** Plaques in periventricular white matter, optic nerve, brainstem, spinal cord.
*   **Patterns:** Relapsing-Remitting (RRMS) is most common. Secondary Progressive (SPMS) follows.

### Pathophysiological Cascade
**[Flow: Demyelination & Conduction Block]**
1.  **Initial Insult:** **T-Cell Infiltration** across BBB.
2.  **Compensatory Response:** **Remyelination**. Oligodendrocyte precursors attempt to repair myelin (shadow plaques).
3.  **Maladaptive Consequence:**
    *   **Conduction Slowing:** Loss of saltatory conduction (myelin insulates axon).
    *   **Conduction Block:** Action potential fizzles out before reaching terminal.
    *   **Axonal Transection:** Unprotected axons degenerate.
4.  **Symptom Mechanism:**
    *   **Uhthoff's Phenomenon:** Heat opens K+ channels $\rightarrow$ faster leak of current from demyelinated axon $\rightarrow$ conduction block worsens.
    *   **Lhermitte's Sign:** Mechanical stretching of demyelinated dorsal columns triggers ectopic firing.

### Clinical Correlations
*   **Optic Neuritis:** Painful monocular vision loss.
*   **INO (Internuclear Ophthalmoplegia):** MLF lesion $\rightarrow$ impaired adduction on lateral gaze.
*   **Lhermitte's Sign:** Electric shock sensation down spine on neck flexion.
*   **Uhthoff's Phenomenon:** Worsening symptoms with heat.

### Diagnostic Biomarkers
*   **MRI:** Periventricular plaques (Dawson's fingers).
*   **CSF:** **Oligoclonal Bands** (IgG), elevated IgG index.
