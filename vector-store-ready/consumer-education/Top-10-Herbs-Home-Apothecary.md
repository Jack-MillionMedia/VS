# How to Use Your Home Apothecary

## Basic Preparation Methods

### Making Herbal Tea (Infusion)
**Best for:** Leaves and flowers (chamomile, peppermint, lemon balm, lavender)

1. Use 1-2 teaspoons dried herb per cup water
2. Boil water, then pour over herbs
3. Cover and steep 5-10 minutes (cover preserves volatile oils!)
4. Strain and drink
5. Add honey or lemon if desired

**Pro tip:** Cover your tea while steeping to prevent medicinal volatile oils from escaping!

---

### Making Herbal Decoction
**Best for:** Roots, barks, and hard plant parts (ginger, turmeric)

1. Use 1-2 teaspoons dried herb per cup water
2. Add herbs to cold water
3. Bring to boil, then reduce to simmer
4. Simmer covered for 10-20 minutes
5. Strain and drink

---

### Storage Guidelines

**Dried herbs:**
- Store in airtight glass jars
- Keep in cool, dark place
- Label with name and date
- Use within 6-12 months for best potency
- Check for color, aroma (faded = expired)

**Fresh herbs:**
- Refrigerate in damp paper towel
- Use within 1-2 weeks
- Can freeze ginger and turmeric root

**Essential oils:**
- Store in dark glass bottles
- Keep tightly sealed
- Store in cool place
- Last 1-2 years for citrus; 3-5 years for most others
