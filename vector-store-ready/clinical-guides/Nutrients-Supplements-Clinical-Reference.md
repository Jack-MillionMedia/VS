## Clinical Overview

### Foundation of Nutritional Medicine

**Key Principle:**
Optimal health requires adequate intake of essential nutrients. Modern diets often lack sufficient vitamins and minerals due to:
- Processed foods
- Nutrient-depleted soils
- Increased stress and toxic exposures
- Genetic variations affecting nutrient metabolism (MTHFR, etc.)
- Medications depleting nutrients (statins → CoQ10, metformin → B12)

**Clinical Approach:**
- Assess nutritional status (history, labs)
- Correct deficiencies with therapeutic dosing
- Maintain optimal levels with preventive dosing
- Use nutrients to support specific health conditions

## Fat-Soluble Vitamins

### Vitamin A (Retinol and Carotenoids) (Grade A)

**Forms:**
- **Preformed Vitamin A (Retinol):** Animal sources (liver, fish, dairy, eggs)
- **Provitamin A (Carotenoids):** Plant sources (carrots, sweet potatoes, leafy greens)
  - Beta-carotene converts to retinol (conversion less efficient in older adults, vegetarians, zinc-deficient)

**Functions:**
- Vision (retinal function, night vision)
- Immune function (epithelial tissue integrity)
- Cell differentiation and growth
- Reproduction
- Skin and mucosal health

**Clinical Applications:**
- Vision disorders (night blindness, dry eyes)
- Immune support (recurrent infections)
- Skin conditions (acne, keratosis pilaris, dry skin)
- Gut health (leaky gut, IBD)
- Respiratory health (frequent respiratory infections)

**Deficiency Symptoms:**
- Night blindness, dry eyes
- Recurrent infections
- Dry skin, keratosis pilaris (bumpy skin)
- Poor wound healing
- Weak tooth enamel

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 900 mcg RAE men, 700 mcg RAE women
- **Therapeutic:** 5000-10,000 IU daily (short-term)
- **Upper Limit:** 10,000 IU daily (chronic use; avoid in pregnancy)
- **Carotenoids:** 10,000-25,000 IU daily (safe; no toxicity)

**Food Sources:**
- **Preformed:** Liver, cod liver oil, egg yolk, butter
- **Carotenoids:** Carrots, sweet potatoes, spinach, kale, squash

**Safety:**
- **Toxicity:** >10,000 IU daily long-term (liver damage, bone loss, birth defects)
- **Pregnancy:** Avoid >5000 IU daily (teratogenic)
- **Monitor:** Liver function if high doses
- **Carotenoids:** Very safe (no toxicity; may cause harmless orange skin)

---

### Vitamin D (Cholecalciferol) (Grade A)

**Function:**
- Calcium absorption and bone health
- Immune function (innate and adaptive)
- Muscle function
- Cardiovascular health
- Mood and mental health
- Cell differentiation and growth

**Clinical Applications:**
- Bone health (osteoporosis prevention, fracture reduction)
- Immune support (respiratory infections, COVID-19, autoimmune)
- Mood disorders (depression, SAD)
- Cardiovascular disease prevention
- Cancer prevention (especially colorectal)
- Type 2 diabetes (improves insulin sensitivity)

**Deficiency:**
- Extremely common (>50% of population <30 ng/mL)
- Risk factors: Limited sun, darker skin, obesity, older age, malabsorption

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 600-800 IU daily (insufficient for optimal levels)
- **Therapeutic:** 2000-5000 IU daily (maintenance)
- **Deficiency Correction:** 5000-10,000 IU daily (short-term, under supervision)
- **Target Level:** 40-60 ng/mL (optimal for immune and overall health)

**Testing:**
- **Serum 25(OH)D:** Gold standard
- **Optimal:** 40-60 ng/mL (some experts recommend up to 80 ng/mL)
- **Deficiency:** <30 ng/mL

**Safety:**
- Very safe at physiological doses
- **Toxicity:** Rare (<40,000 IU/day long-term)
- Monitor levels if using high doses

**Cofactors:**
- **Vitamin K2:** Essential for proper calcium metabolism (directs calcium to bones, not arteries)
- **Magnesium:** Required for vitamin D activation

---

### Vitamin E (Tocopherols and Tocotrienols) (Grade B+)

**Forms:**
- **Alpha-tocopherol:** Most common in supplements
- **Mixed tocopherols:** Alpha, beta, gamma, delta (preferred)
- **Tocotrienols:** Superior antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects (especially delta-tocotrienol)

**Functions:**
- Fat-soluble antioxidant
- Protects cell membranes from oxidation
- Cardiovascular health (prevents LDL oxidation)
- Nervous system health
- Immune function
- Skin health

**Clinical Applications:**
- Cardiovascular disease (antioxidant protection, prevents LDL oxidation)
- Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (delta-tocotrienol especially effective)
- Diabetic retinopathy (slows progression)
- Skin health (wound healing, anti-aging)
- Neuropathy (nerve protection)

**Deficiency:**
- Rare (fat malabsorption, genetic conditions)
- Symptoms: Neuropathy, muscle weakness, vision changes, anemia

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 15 mg (22.5 IU)
- **Therapeutic:** 400 IU mixed tocopherols + tocotrienols
- **Avoid:** Synthetic forms (dl-alpha-tocopherol acetate) - may have toxic effects

**Food Sources:**
- Wheat germ oil, sunflower oil, almonds, hazelnuts, spinach, avocado

**Safety:**
- Generally safe
- **High doses (>1000 IU):** May increase bleeding risk (monitor with anticoagulants)
- **Choose natural forms:** d-alpha-tocopherol and mixed tocopherols/tocotrienols

---

### Vitamin K (K1 and K2) (Grade B+)

**Forms:**
- **K1 (Phylloquinone):** Leafy greens, blood clotting
- **K2 (Menaquinones):** Fermented foods, bone and cardiovascular health
  - **MK-4:** Animal sources (short half-life)
  - **MK-7:** Natto, fermented foods (longer half-life, preferred)

**Functions:**
- **K1:** Blood clotting (coagulation cascade)
- **K2:** Calcium metabolism (activates proteins that direct calcium to bones, not arteries)
- Bone health
- Cardiovascular health (prevents arterial calcification)

**Clinical Applications:**
- **K2 MK-7:** Osteoporosis prevention, cardiovascular disease prevention
- Essential cofactor for vitamin D supplementation

**Dosing:**
- **K1 RDA:** 90-120 mcg
- **K2 (MK-7):** 100-200 mcg daily (therapeutic)

**Safety:**
- Very safe
- **Contraindication:** Warfarin (antagonizes vitamin K) - do NOT supplement without prescriber approval

## Water-Soluble Vitamins

### Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid) (Grade A)

**Functions:**
- Collagen synthesis (skin, blood vessels, bones, connective tissue)
- Antioxidant
- Immune function (supports neutrophils, lymphocytes)
- Neurotransmitter synthesis
- Iron absorption
- Wound healing

**Clinical Applications:**
- Immune support (cold/flu prevention and treatment)
- Cardiovascular health (endothelial function, collagen in blood vessels)
- Wound healing
- Skin health (collagen production, anti-aging)
- Iron-deficiency anemia (enhances iron absorption)
- Scurvy prevention

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 75-90 mg
- **Optimal Prevention:** 500-1000 mg daily (divided doses)
- **Acute Illness (Cold/Flu):** 1000-2000 mg every 2-4 hours (to bowel tolerance)
- **High-Dose (IV):** Used in hospital settings (sepsis, cancer support)

**Food Sources:**
- Citrus fruits, berries, kiwi, bell peppers, broccoli, tomatoes

**Safety:**
- Very safe
- **Side Effects:** Diarrhea at high doses (reduce dose)
- **Kidney Stones:** Theoretical risk in predisposed individuals (ensure adequate hydration)

**Synergies:**
- **Bioflavonoids:** Enhance vitamin C activity
- **Vitamin E:** Vitamin C regenerates oxidized vitamin E

### B-Complex Vitamins (Grade A)

**Overview:**
- 8 essential B vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B7, B9, B12)
- Water-soluble (not stored; require daily intake)
- Essential for energy metabolism, neurotransmitter synthesis, DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation

---

**B1 (Thiamine):**
- **Function:** Energy metabolism, nerve function
- **Deficiency:** Beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff (alcoholics)
- **Dosing:** 50-100 mg daily (therapeutic)
- **Special Form:** Benfotiamine (fat-soluble, superior for neuropathy and circulation)

**B2 (Riboflavin):**
- **Function:** Energy metabolism, antioxidant (glutathione regeneration)
- **Deficiency:** Cracked lips, tongue inflammation
- **Dosing:** 50-400 mg daily (migraine prevention: 400 mg)

**B3 (Niacin / Niacinamide):**
- **Function:** Energy metabolism, DNA repair, cholesterol metabolism
- **Forms:**
  - Niacin (nicotinic acid): Lowers cholesterol (LDL, triglycerides), raises HDL
  - Niacinamide: No cholesterol effect, no flushing
- **Dosing (Niacin):** 1-2 g daily (cholesterol); causes flushing
- **Dosing (Niacinamide):** 500-1000 mg daily (no flushing)

**B5 (Pantothenic Acid):**
- **Function:** Adrenal function, stress response, energy
- **Dosing:** 250-500 mg daily

**B6 (Pyridoxine / P5P):**
- **Function:** Neurotransmitter synthesis (serotonin, dopamine, GABA, norepinephrine)
- **Forms:** Pyridoxine (requires conversion), P5P (active form)
- **Dosing:** 25-100 mg daily (therapeutic)
- **Caution:** >200 mg/day long-term may cause peripheral neuropathy

**B7 (Biotin):**
- **Function:** Hair, skin, nails; glucose metabolism
- **Dosing:** 2.5-5 mg daily (hair/skin/nails)

**B9 (Folate / Methylfolate):**
- **Function:** DNA synthesis, cell division, homocysteine metabolism, neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Forms:**
  - Folic acid (synthetic - requires conversion via MTHFR enzyme)
  - **Methylfolate (5-MTHF):** Active form, bypasses MTHFR (PREFERRED)
- **MTHFR Polymorphism:** ~50% of population has genetic variants affecting folate metabolism
- **Deficiency:** Anemia, neural tube defects (pregnancy), elevated homocysteine, depression
- **Dosing:** 400-1000 mcg methylfolate daily
- **Pregnancy:** 600-800 mcg (prenatal)

**B12 (Cobalamin / Methylcobalamin):**
- **Function:** DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, myelin synthesis, energy, neurotransmitter synthesis
- **Forms:**
  - Cyanocobalamin (synthetic, requires conversion)
  - **Methylcobalamin (Methyl-B12):** Active form (PREFERRED)
  - Adenosylcobalamin (active form, less common)
- **Deficiency:** Pernicious anemia, neuropathy, cognitive decline, fatigue, depression
  - Risk factors: Vegan/vegetarian, elderly, malabsorption, metformin use
- **Dosing:** 500-1000 mcg daily (oral); 1000 mcg IM (injections for severe deficiency)
- **Testing:** Serum B12, methylmalonic acid (MMA), homocysteine

---

**Methylated B-Complex (Preferred):**
- **Why:** ~50% of people have MTHFR or related polymorphisms affecting B vitamin metabolism
- **Forms:** Methylfolate (B9) + Methylcobalamin (B12) + P5P (B6)
- **Clinical Applications:**
  - Brain fog, fatigue, depression
  - Elevated homocysteine (cardiovascular risk)
  - Cognitive decline prevention
  - Neurotransmitter support

## Essential Minerals

### Magnesium (Grade A)

**Functions:**
- Cofactor in 300+ enzymatic reactions
- Energy production (ATP synthesis)
- Muscle and nerve function
- Bone health
- Cardiovascular health (vasodilation, blood pressure)
- Sleep (GABA modulation)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Hypertension (lowers blood pressure)
- Arrhythmias (stabilizes cardiac rhythm)
- Migraine prevention (400-600 mg daily)
- Sleep disorders (promotes relaxation and sleep)
- Anxiety and stress
- Muscle cramps and spasms
- Constipation (magnesium citrate)
- Cognitive function (magnesium threonate for brain)

**Deficiency:**
- Extremely common (50%+ of population)
- Symptoms: Muscle cramps, fatigue, weakness, arrhythmias, hypertension, insomnia

**Forms:**
- **Citrate:** Good absorption, mild laxative effect
- **Glycinate/Bisglycinate:** Excellent absorption, calming (glycine), best for sleep
- **Threonate (Magtein):** Brain-penetrating, cognitive support
- **Oxide:** Poor absorption, strong laxative
- **Taurate:** Cardiovascular support
- **Malate:** Energy, fibromyalgia

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 310-420 mg
- **Therapeutic:** 400-800 mg daily (divided doses)
- **Sleep:** 400-600 mg before bed (glycinate or threonate)
- **Migraine:** 400-600 mg daily

**Testing:**
- **RBC Magnesium:** More accurate than serum (serum often normal despite tissue deficiency)

**Safety:**
- Very safe
- **Side Effect:** Diarrhea (reduce dose; choose better-absorbed forms)
- **Caution:** Severe renal failure (accumulation risk)

---

### Zinc (Grade A)

**Functions:**
- Immune function (T-cells, antibody production)
- Wound healing
- Protein synthesis
- Testosterone production
- DNA synthesis
- Taste and smell
- Antiviral (inhibits viral replication)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Cold duration reduction (lozenges, start within 24 hours)
- Immune support
- Low testosterone and male fertility
- Acne and skin health
- Wound healing
- Age-related macular degeneration (AMD)

**Deficiency:**
- Common (vegetarians, elderly, malabsorption)
- Symptoms: Frequent infections, poor wound healing, hair loss, low testosterone, altered taste/smell

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 8-11 mg
- **Therapeutic:** 30-50 mg daily (short-term)
- **Maintenance:** 15-30 mg daily
- **Cold (Lozenges):** 13-25 mg every 2-3 hours (max 5-7 days)

**Safety:**
- Generally safe
- **Long-term high doses (>50 mg):** May deplete copper (monitor; consider zinc:copper ratio)
- **Side Effects:** Nausea (take with food)

---

### Iron (Grade A)

**Functions:**
- Oxygen transport (hemoglobin, myoglobin)
- Energy production
- Neurotransmitter synthesis
- Immune function

**Clinical Applications:**
- Iron-deficiency anemia
- Fatigue (even without anemia, low ferritin causes fatigue)
- Restless legs syndrome (RLS) - ferritin <50 ng/mL
- ADHD (low ferritin linked to symptoms)

**Deficiency:**
- Common (menstruating women, vegetarians, blood loss, malabsorption)
- Symptoms: Fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath, cold intolerance, restless legs

**Testing:**
- **Serum Ferritin:** Best indicator of iron stores
  - **Optimal:** 50-100 ng/mL (some conditions require higher)
- **Serum Iron, TIBC, Transferrin Saturation, CBC:** Comprehensive iron panel

**Dosing:**
- **Elemental Iron:** 30-60 mg daily (therapeutic)
- **Forms:**
  - Ferrous sulfate: Most common, GI upset common
  - Ferrous bisglycinate: Better absorbed, less GI upset (PREFERRED)
  - Ferrous fumarate, ferrous gluconate

**Timing:**
- Take on empty stomach for best absorption (if tolerated)
- **Cofactors:** Vitamin C (enhances absorption)
- **Avoid with:** Calcium, tea, coffee (inhibit absorption)

**Safety:**
- Generally safe when deficient
- **Side Effects:** Constipation, nausea, dark stools
- **Toxicity:** Rare in adults; dangerous in children (keep out of reach)
- **Hereditary Hemochromatosis:** Avoid supplementation

---

### Selenium (Grade B)

**Functions:**
- Antioxidant (glutathione peroxidase cofactor)
- Thyroid hormone metabolism
- Immune function
- Fertility

**Clinical Applications:**
- Thyroid health (Hashimoto's thyroiditis - reduces antibodies)
- Cancer prevention
- Cardiovascular health
- Male fertility

**Dosing:**
- **RDA:** 55 mcg
- **Therapeutic:** 200 mcg daily

**Safety:**
- **Upper Limit:** 400 mcg daily
- **Toxicity (Selenosis):** >400 mcg/day chronic (hair loss, nail brittleness, GI upset, neurological)

## Amino Acids and Derivatives

### L-Theanine (Grade B+)

**Source:** Green tea, especially matcha

**Functions:**
- Increases alpha brain waves (calm focus)
- Modulates neurotransmitters (GABA, serotonin, dopamine)
- Anxiolytic (anti-anxiety)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Anxiety and stress
- Focus and attention (especially with caffeine)
- ADHD (children and adults)
- Sleep support (calming without sedation)

**Dosing:**
- **General:** 100-200 mg daily
- **Stress/Anxiety:** 200-400 mg divided
- **With Caffeine:** 200 mg L-theanine + 100 mg caffeine (optimal ratio 2:1)

**Safety:** Excellent; no known side effects

---

### L-Tyrosine (Grade B)

**Function:**
- Precursor to dopamine, norepinephrine, thyroid hormones, CoQ10, melanin

**Clinical Applications:**
- Chronic stress and fatigue
- Cognitive performance under stress
- Depression (dopamine support)
- Thyroid support (T3/T4 synthesis)

**Dosing:**
- 500-1500 mg daily (divided doses)
- Take on empty stomach

**Safety:**
- Generally safe
- **Caution:** Hyperthyroidism, medications for Parkinson's

---

### L-Lysine (Grade C+)

**Functions:**
- Collagen production
- Iron uptake
- Immunity
- Epigenetic gene expression

**Clinical Applications:**
- Cold sores (herpes simplex) - inhibits arginine uptake by virus
- Connective tissue health
- Anemia (supports iron uptake)
- Immunity

**Dosing:**
- **General:** 500-1000 mg daily
- **Cold Sores:** 1000-3000 mg daily during outbreak

**Synergy:** Lemon balm (topical) for cold sores

---

### L-Arginine and L-Citrulline (Grade B+)

**Functions:**
- Nitric oxide precursors
- Vasodilation (blood flow)
- Cardiovascular health
- Sexual function (erectile function)
- Exercise performance

**Clinical Applications:**
- Erectile dysfunction
- Cardiovascular health (angina, peripheral artery disease)
- Athletic performance
- Hypertension

**L-Citrulline:** More effective than L-arginine (better absorbed, converts to arginine in body)

**Dosing:**
- **L-Arginine:** 3-5 g daily (divided)
- **L-Citrulline:** 3-6 g daily (PREFERRED)

**Synergy:** Pycnogenol (120 mg) + L-citrulline (3 g) for ED

**Safety:**
- Well-tolerated
- **Caution:** Recent MI, herpes (arginine may trigger outbreaks)

---

### 5-HTP (5-Hydroxytryptophan) (Grade C+)

**Function:**
- Precursor to serotonin and melatonin

**Clinical Applications:**
- Depression and mood support
- Insomnia (serotonin → melatonin)
- Appetite regulation

**Dosing:**
- 50-100 mg before bed (sleep) or BID-TID (mood)

**Safety:**
- **Caution:** SSRIs, MAOIs (risk of serotonin syndrome)
- **Side Effects:** Nausea (start low)

---

### GABA (Gamma-Aminobutyric Acid) (Grade B)

**Function:**
- Primary inhibitory neurotransmitter (calming)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Anxiety and stress
- Insomnia (especially anxiety-driven)
- Overactive mind and rumination

**Dosing:**
- 500-1000 mg before bed or as needed

**Debate:** Does oral GABA cross blood-brain barrier? (Evidence suggests it does act centrally despite molecular size)

---

### Glycine (Grade B)

**Functions:**
- Inhibitory neurotransmitter
- Collagen synthesis
- Detoxification (conjugation)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Sleep quality (improves deep sleep)
- Anxiety
- Skin and connective tissue health
- Detoxification support

**Dosing:**
- **Sleep:** 3-5 g before bed

**Safety:** Excellent; very well-tolerated

---

### N-Acetylcysteine (NAC) (Grade A)

**Function:**
- Precursor to glutathione (master antioxidant)
- Mucolytic (thins mucus)
- Detoxification

**Clinical Applications:**
- Respiratory health (COPD, chronic bronchitis, sinusitis)
- Liver detoxification (acetaminophen overdose antidote)
- Mental health (OCD, trichotillomania, NAC shows promise)
- Antioxidant support
- Post-viral recovery (long COVID)

**Dosing:**
- 600-1200 mg BID

**Safety:**
- Well-tolerated
- **Side Effects:** GI upset (take with food)

**Regulatory:** FDA threatened reclassification as drug (controversial; advocacy ongoing)

## Antioxidants and Polyphenols

### Quercetin (Grade B)

**Functions:**
- Flavonoid antioxidant
- Anti-inflammatory
- Antihistamine (mast cell stabilizer)
- Antiviral
- Zinc ionophore (helps zinc enter cells)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Allergies (seasonal allergic rhinitis)
- Viral infections (COVID-19, flu) - combine with zinc
- Inflammation
- Cardiovascular health

**Dosing:**
- **General:** 500-1000 mg daily
- **Acute Illness:** 1000 mg BID-TID

**Food Sources:** Capers, onions, apples, berries

---

### CoQ10 (Ubiquinone / Ubiquinol) (Grade A)

**Functions:**
- Mitochondrial ATP production (energy)
- Antioxidant

**Clinical Applications:**
- Heart failure (reduces hospitalizations and mortality)
- Statin-induced myopathy (statins deplete CoQ10 - ALL statin users should supplement)
- Hypertension (modest blood pressure reduction)
- Mitochondrial support (fatigue, post-viral recovery)
- Fertility (egg and sperm quality)

**Forms:**
- **Ubiquinone:** Standard form
- **Ubiquinol:** Reduced form (better absorbed, especially in elderly or those with poor absorption)

**Dosing:**
- **General:** 100-200 mg daily
- **Heart Failure or Statin Use:** 200-300 mg daily

**Timing:** Take with fat-containing meal (improves absorption)

---

### Resveratrol (Grade B)

**Function:**
- Polyphenol antioxidant
- Activates sirtuins (longevity genes)
- Anti-inflammatory

**Clinical Applications:**
- Anti-aging
- Cardiovascular health
- Cognitive health
- Metabolic health (insulin sensitivity)

**Dosing:**
- 200-500 mg daily

**Food Sources:** Red wine (small amounts), grapes, berries, peanuts

---

### Curcumin (Turmeric) (Grade B+)

**Functions:**
- Potent anti-inflammatory (COX-2 inhibitor)
- Antioxidant
- Neuroprotective
- Anti-amyloid (Alzheimer's)

**Clinical Applications:**
- Inflammatory conditions (arthritis, IBD)
- Pain management
- Cognitive health (Alzheimer's prevention)
- Metabolic health

**Dosing:**
- 1-2 g daily (with piperine or fat for absorption)

**Forms:** Look for enhanced absorption formulations (with piperine, liposomal, Meriva)

---

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA) (Grade A)

**Functions:**
- Anti-inflammatory (specialized pro-resolving mediators)
- Brain structure (DHA is major component of neuronal membranes)
- Cardiovascular health
- Mood regulation

**Clinical Applications:**
- Cardiovascular disease (triglyceride reduction, heart failure)
- Depression (EPA particularly effective)
- Cognitive health (brain volume preservation)
- Inflammatory conditions (autoimmune, chronic inflammation)

**Dosing:**
- **General Health:** 1-2 g EPA+DHA daily
- **Therapeutic (Depression, Inflammation):** 2-3 g EPA+DHA daily
- **High Triglycerides:** 3-4 g EPA+DHA daily

**Testing:** Omega-3 Index (target >8%)

**Sources:**
- Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, anchovies, mackerel)
- Fish oil
- Krill oil (excellent bioavailability)
- Algae oil (vegan DHA/EPA)
- Ahiflower oil (plant-based alternative)

## Clinical Application Protocols

### Protocol 1: Foundational Daily Multivitamin/Multimineral

**Rationale:** Cover nutritional gaps; provide baseline support

**Components:**
- Methylated B-Complex (including methylfolate, methylcobalamin)
- Vitamin D3: 2000-5000 IU
- Vitamin K2 (MK-7): 100 mcg
- Magnesium: 200-400 mg
- Zinc: 15-30 mg
- Selenium: 200 mcg
- Mixed tocopherols and tocotrienols (Vitamin E): 400 IU

**Recommendation:** Choose high-quality multivitamin or build custom protocol

---

### Protocol 2: Cardiovascular Support

- Omega-3s: 2-3 g EPA+DHA daily
- CoQ10: 200-300 mg daily (especially if on statins)
- Magnesium: 400-600 mg daily
- Vitamin D: Optimize to 50-60 ng/mL
- Vitamin C: 1000 mg daily
- Vitamin E (mixed tocopherols): 400 IU daily

---

### Protocol 3: Cognitive Enhancement

- Methylated B-Complex: Daily
- Omega-3s: 2 g EPA+DHA daily
- Magnesium Threonate: 1500-2000 mg daily
- Vitamin D: Optimize levels
- Acetyl-L-Carnitine: 1000 mg daily

---

### Protocol 4: Immune Support

- Vitamin D: 2000-5000 IU daily (maintain 40-60 ng/mL)
- Vitamin C: 1000 mg daily (increase to 2000 mg every 2-4 hours if acute illness)
- Zinc: 15-30 mg daily (acute: lozenges 15-25 mg every 2-3 hours max 5-7 days)
- Quercetin: 500-1000 mg daily
- Vitamin A: 5000 IU daily

---

### Protocol 5: Energy and Mitochondrial Support

- CoQ10: 200-300 mg daily
- Acetyl-L-Carnitine: 1000-1500 mg daily
- Alpha-Lipoic Acid: 300-600 mg daily
- B-Complex: Daily (especially B1, B2, B3, B5)
- Magnesium: 400 mg daily
- Iron: If deficient (check ferritin)

## Safety and Interactions Reference

### Nutrients that Enhance Anticoagulants (Bleeding Risk)

- Vitamin E (>400 IU)
- Omega-3s (high doses)
- Garlic (high doses)
- Ginkgo

**Management:** Monitor INR; avoid high doses without medical supervision

---

### Nutrients Depleted by Common Medications

**Statins:**
- CoQ10 (ALL statin users should supplement 200-300 mg/day)

**Metformin:**
- B12 (supplement methylcobalamin 500-1000 mcg/day; monitor levels)

**PPIs (Proton Pump Inhibitors):**
- B12, magnesium, calcium, iron

**Diuretics:**
- Magnesium, potassium, B vitamins

**Oral Contraceptives:**
- B vitamins (especially B6, folate), magnesium

---

## Quality and Selection Guidelines

**Look For:**
- Third-party testing (ConsumerLab, NSF, USP)
- GMP certification
- Transparent labeling (forms of nutrients clearly stated)
- Appropriate dosing (therapeutic levels, not just RDA)
- No unnecessary fillers, dyes, allergens

**Preferred Forms:**
- Methylated B vitamins (methylfolate, methylcobalamin, P5P)
- Chelated minerals (bisglycinate, citrate) - better absorption
- Natural vitamin E (mixed tocopherols/tocotrienols, not synthetic dl-alpha)
- CoQ10 as ubiquinol (or ubiquinone in younger adults)
